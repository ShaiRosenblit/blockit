## Candidate L5-1 — Drill one occupied cell before placement
- Lens: 5 (Finite resource)
- Generated by: GPT-5.5
- Rule changes: Puzzle rules, plus: before placing a tray piece, the player may spend 1 Drill to erase exactly one currently occupied board cell. The piece is then placed and normal row/column clears run. The generator forward-simulates both no-drill and drill-before-placement branches and rejects puzzles whose proving line never spends a Drill.
- Win condition: after all tray pieces are placed, board occupancy equals the target pattern exactly.
- Lose condition: no tray pieces remain and board occupancy does not equal the target, or the current state has no legal placement under any remaining Drill spend.
- New question per placement: spend a Drill now, and if so which occupied cell is worth removing before this piece changes the clear graph?
- Dual-purpose mechanic: erasure opens space and can remove bad pre-fill, but it can also remove a cell needed to complete a line clear or final target cell.
- Antagonist: pre-filled cells outside the target that are positioned so ordinary clears alone cannot remove all of them in the available piece count.
- Negative-space constraint: erased non-target cells must stay empty at the end; erasing a target cell creates a refill obligation.
- Order coupling: drilling before a placement can prevent, enable, or redirect the immediate clear, so the same piece sequence with the same Drill spent later gives a different board.
- Resource: Drills; accrue only at puzzle start; spend 1 to erase one occupied cell before a placement; cap 1/2/3/3 by difficulty.

## Candidate L5-2 — Brace one line against the next clear
- Lens: 5 (Finite resource)
- Generated by: GPT-5.5
- Rule changes: Puzzle rules, plus: before placing a piece, the player may spend 1 Brace on one row or one column. After placement, completed rows and columns clear normally except the braced line, which remains filled if completed; the Brace then expires. The generator forward-simulates the chosen braced line as part of the state.
- Win condition: after all tray pieces are placed, board occupancy equals the target pattern exactly.
- Lose condition: no tray pieces remain and board occupancy does not equal the target, or the current state has no legal placement under any remaining Brace spend.
- New question per placement: is this placement worth spending a Brace on a row or column that must survive while another line clears?
- Dual-purpose mechanic: line clears delete unwanted cells, but an unbraced clear can also delete target scaffolding that is needed later.
- Antagonist: rows or columns containing target cells that must temporarily become full while crossing junk lines are cleared.
- Negative-space constraint: a braced full line remains a placement blocker until a later unbraced clear or target finish accounts for it.
- Order coupling: bracing a line before a crossing clear changes which cells survive, so clearing the same row/column pair in a different order yields different reachable targets.
- Resource: Braces; accrue only at puzzle start; spend 1 to protect one selected row or column for the current placement's clear step; cap 1/2/2/3 by difficulty.

- Order coupling: an anchor only affects the next clear that touches it, so placing the anchored cell before or after the relevant clear changes whether it is useful.

## Candidate L5-8 — Bank no-clear placements to force one near-line clear

- Generated by: GPT-5.5
- Rule changes: Puzzle rules, plus: after a placement that produces zero standard row/column clears, gain 1 Pressure up to cap. Before placing, the player may spend 2 Pressure to clear one selected row or column that currently has at least 6 occupied cells, even though it is not full. The forced clear happens before the placement; then placement and normal clears run. The generator forward-simulates Pressure gain and every legal forced clear spend.
- Win condition: after all tray pieces are placed, board occupancy equals the target pattern exactly.
- Lose condition: no tray pieces remain and board occupancy does not equal the target, or the current state has no legal placement under any remaining or earnable Pressure state.

- Dual-purpose mechanic: not clearing is both board congestion and the only way to charge the forced-clear resource.
- Antagonist: six- or seven-cell rows and columns containing bad pre-fill that cannot be completed cleanly without overfilling non-target cells.
- Negative-space constraint: banking Pressure leaves more occupied cells on the board, so the empty cells kept for future piece origins become scarce.
- Order coupling: earning Pressure requires delaying clears, while spending it before a later placement removes cells that would otherwise affect that placement's clear result.
- Resource: Pressure; starts at 0, gains 1 after any placement with zero standard clears, cap 3; spend 2 before placement to clear one selected row or column with at least 6 occupied cells.
