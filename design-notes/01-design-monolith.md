# Monolith — design doc

**Tagline (mechanics-speak)**: each placement must touch the existing filled region (pre-fill seed + previously placed cells); line clears remove block-pre-fill but may fragment the region; win = filled cells exactly match target.

## Rules (precise)

- **Board**: 8×8. Cell categories at any moment:
  - `EMPTY`
  - `SEED` — pre-fill that is part of the target. Initially forms ONE 4-connected component.
  - `BLOCK` — pre-fill that is NOT part of the target. Must be evicted via line clears by end.
  - `PLACED` — player-placed cell.

- **Target `T`** ⊆ {0..7}². `SEED ⊆ T`. End-state requirement: filled cells = `T` exactly AND no `BLOCK` cells remain.

- **Tray**: 3-slot finite, generated by forward simulation (matches `puzzleGenerator.ts` shape).

- **Placement rule (the new constraint, "Touch invariant")**:
  A placement at `(r,c)` with piece footprint `F` is legal iff:
  1. All cells in `F` are `EMPTY`.
  2. At least one cell in `F` is 4-adjacent to a cell of type `SEED` or `PLACED` (i.e., extends the monolith).
  3. After placement and any resulting line clears, the union `SEED ∪ PLACED` is a single 4-connected component (the **Connectivity invariant**).

- **Line clears**: whenever a row or column is fully filled (any combination of `SEED`, `BLOCK`, `PLACED`), all 8 cells of that row/column clear (matches Puzzle behavior). Multiple lines can clear at once.

- **Win**: post-move state has `SEED ∪ PLACED == T` (no `BLOCK` cells remain, all and only `T` cells are filled).

- **Lose**: no legal placement exists for any tray slot at any rotation (same shape as Puzzle/Mirror game-over check).

- **Restart**: re-deal the same generated puzzle (same `T`, `SEED`, `BLOCK`, tray). Matches Puzzle mode UX.

- **Undo**: single-level, mirrors Puzzle mode.

## 7 ingredients (scored)

1. **Bounded scope** (2): finite 8×8, finite tray, fast restart.
2. **Antagonist** (1.5): `BLOCK` cells are the explicit defeat target. Mild penalty because the player can also lose to fragmentation, which is self-inflicted but learnable.
3. **Dual-purpose mechanic** (2): line clears are simultaneously WANTED (delete `BLOCK`) and FEARED (may fragment the monolith).
4. **Order matters** (2): the growing-front shape of the monolith determines which cells are reachable on subsequent placements; permuting the same tray yields different feasible trajectories and different fragmentation risks.
5. **Negative space** (2): cells outside `T` must end empty; furthermore the choice to NOT extend in a direction shapes which `BLOCK`s are reachable for clearing.
6. **Solvability guarantee** (2): forward-simulation generator produces a known solution path before the puzzle is shown.
7. **Structural aha** (2): "the monolith's path threads through line clears that delete blocks without disconnecting the monolith" — a system-level insight, not a flavor twist.

**Total ≈ 13.5/14.**

## Anti-pattern tests (all PASS)

- **Mirror disease** — FAIL? No. The Touch invariant changes which placements are legal as a function of the *running history* (the current monolith's perimeter), not as a deterministic function of the current placement. Each move asks: "of legal pieces × rotations × origins along the monolith's frontier, which extends toward `T` and avoids fragmenting clears?" That is a *new* per-move question, not a function of an existing decision.
- **Breathe disease** — No. The win-condition `SEED ∪ PLACED = T` is not implied by the other rules; a player can satisfy "Touch + Connectivity" forever without ever reaching `T` (e.g., by extending into non-T cells, which is illegal because non-T cells must end empty… actually placing on non-T cells is allowed *temporarily* if a future line clear removes them, so the "stay in T" rule isn't auto-satisfied). The Touch invariant alone does not imply connectivity post-clear.
- **Pipeline disease** — No. Touch+Connectivity REMOVE some placements (you can't place far from the monolith, can't make fragmenting clears). The COMPENSATING decision dimension is "frontier routing": at every move, the player chooses *which edge of the monolith to extend from* and *whether to set up a clear that removes a block without fragmenting*. Net decision density per move > Classic.
- **Scar disease** — No. `SEED` and `BLOCK` placements are deterministic per puzzle (generator-fixed). No random uncontrollable punishment. Fragmenting clears are punishments the player can foresee (connectivity check is local/learnable).

## Generator strategy (pseudocode)

```text
generateMonolith(difficulty):
  for attempt in 1..MAX_ATTEMPTS:
    T = randomConnectedShape(targetCellRange[difficulty])   # 4-connected, |T| ∈ [10, 22]
    SEED = pickConnectedSubset(T, seedCellRange[difficulty]) # 4-connected, |SEED| ∈ [3, 6]
    BLOCKS = pickRandomBlocks(complementOfT, blockCount[difficulty]) # NOT in T
    TRAY = []
    monolith = SEED
    placedSoFar = ∅
    while monolith ∪ placedSoFar != T:
      legalCandidates = pieces × rotations × origins where:
        - footprint ⊆ EMPTY
        - footprint ⊆ T \ (SEED ∪ placedSoFar)   # only place in target cells
        - touches monolith ∪ placedSoFar
        - post-placement (no clears expected since placement is in T) keeps connectivity
      choose one placement; append piece to TRAY; mark cells PLACED; update monolith.
    if |TRAY| in trayLengthRange[difficulty] and BLOCKS were preserved (not auto-cleared during sim):
      # for v1 we don't trigger clears in the solution; clears are pure FEAR for player.
      # later: include solutions that need a deliberate clear to remove a block.
      return { T, SEED, BLOCKS, TRAY }
  return fallback puzzle
```

V1 simplification: solution path does not trigger clears; `BLOCK` cells are placed in rows/columns that the SOLUTION naturally completes via player placement (so they are deleted by line clears in the player's eventual playthrough), OR they are placed where the player must complete a line including them. For v1 we'll keep `BLOCK` count low (0–2 for easy) and have the tray include pieces that complete lines through blocks.

Actually for cleanest v1, blocks are placed in rows/cols such that the solution forward-sim's placements DO trigger a clear that includes the block. I'll iterate during implementation if v1 is too tame.

**Win-state oracle (deterministic)**: `SEED ∪ PLACED == T` (set equality on cell coords) AND `BLOCK == ∅`.

**Quality filters**:
- |T| ≥ 10 (else trivial)
- |TRAY| ≥ 4 (else 2–3-move puzzle, too short)
- ≥1 forced "tight choice" (a placement where only 1 piece+rotation legally fits the frontier)
- BLOCK count ≥ 1 in normal/hard (so the dual-purpose clear is exercised)

**Rejection ratio estimate**: 5–10× per accepted instance (forward sim is straightforward; rejections come from `T` shapes with no valid SEED, or trays that overflow length budget).

## Worked example (small)

Target T (8×8, 0=outside T, X=in T):
```
. . . . . . . .
. . . X X X . .
. . . X . X . .
. . . X X X . .
. . . . . . . .
. . . . . . . .
. . . . . . . .
. . . . . . . .
```

|T| = 8 cells (small example).

SEED = {(1,3), (2,3), (3,3)} (3 cells, 4-connected, ⊆ T).

BLOCK = {(1,7)} (one block on row 1; player must complete row 1 to evict it).

Forward-sim solution:
- Place piece₁ = "I3-horiz" at (1,4..6) → fills (1,4)(1,5)(1,6). Monolith = SEED ∪ {(1,4)(1,5)(1,6)}. Connected. Row 1 has filled = SEED's (1,3) + (1,4)(1,5)(1,6) + BLOCK (1,7) → wait, that's 5 cells. (1,0)(1,1)(1,2) are empty. Row 1 not yet complete.
- Place piece₂ = "I3-horiz" at (1,0..2) → fills row 1 entirely → row 1 clears! All cells in row 1 removed: SEED (1,3), placed (1,0..2), placed (1,4..6), BLOCK (1,7). Monolith = SEED \ {(1,3)} ∪ {} = {(2,3)(3,3)}. Still connected (vertical pair). BLOCK gone. T not yet matched.
- Place piece₃ = some piece covering (2,5)(3,5) ... etc. Continue extending.

Worked transcript (per-move questions):
- M1: "Where to extend SEED rightward? piece₁ I3 fills (1,4..6); touches (1,3) ✓; cells in T ✓; row 1 not complete yet ✓; legal."
- M2: "Will placing piece₂ at (1,0..2) trigger a clear? Yes, row 1 complete. After clear, will SEED stay connected? SEED's (1,3) clears, leaving (2,3)(3,3) connected. ✓. Block evicted."
- M3: ... fills (1,5) area again to match T's row-1 cells? But T's (1,3..5) are part of T and were just cleared. Need to refill them. Question: "How do I extend the (2,3)(3,3) monolith back into row 1's (1,3..5)?"

The transcript demonstrates: M1 = extend, M2 = trigger-clear-without-fragmenting, M3 = refill-cleared-target-cells. Three different questions.
